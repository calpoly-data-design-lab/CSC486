## Learning Objectives

This course is designed to teach you the fundamentals of the feild of HCI so that by the end of the course you will be capable of enaging with contemporary scholarship in HCI, and conduct HCI research in either academic or applied settings. This includes the abilities to:

- Explain fundamentals of human-computer interaction.  
- Identify human capabilities and limitations for interaction with computers.
- Use sensors and actuators suitable for human-computer interaction purposes to provide alternatives to traditional input/output devices.  
- Contrast human-computer interaction spaces, styles, and paradigms.  
- Explain constraints related to environment, devices, and tasks.
- Assess natural user interface capabilities and challenges.
- Explain potential applications for affective, pervasive, or social computing and other emerging technologies.  
- Apply human-computer interaction principles and practices to the development of interactive applications as a member of a team.  
- Analyze and evaluate the user experience of interactive systems using qualitative and quantitative methods.  
- Prepare and deliver effective technical oral presentations.
